Uses of this rubric

Before the task During the process When assessing
Students read the rubric to understand what is expected and how to organise their work. Students use it to review their draft and identify what to improve before submitting. The teacher, the student themselves, or a peer uses it to assess and give specific feedback.

Teaching note: a useful rubric describes observable behaviours, not generic judgements. "Explains ideas clearly with their own examples" is a useful descriptor; "demonstrates understanding of the topic" is not, because it doesn't describe what the student does or how to recognise it. The difference between level 3 and level 4 must be clear enough for students to place themselves without the teacher's help.
